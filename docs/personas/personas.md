# Swim Tracker User Personas

> **Purpose:** These 5 personas represent real swimmer archetypes. Use them to test every feature before shipping. Ask: "Would [Persona] understand/need/use this?"

---

## Persona 1: Comeback Claire
**The Returning Swimmer**

### Demographics
- **Age:** 34
- **Location:** Bristol
- **Occupation:** Marketing Manager
- **Swimming Background:** Swam competitively at county level, 15 years off, recently rejoined local leisure centre

### Goals & Motivations
- Get back in shape without injury
- Track if she's actually improving (doubts progress week-to-week)
- Understand what "good" looks like for her age/fitness level
- Fit swimming around demanding work schedule and commute

### Pain Points & Frustrations
- Doesn't remember proper technique anymore
- No idea if 2:30/100m pace is decent or rubbish
- Can't tell if fatigue is normal or sign of overtraining
- Feels daft asking pool lifeguards for coaching advice
- Compares herself to county-level teenage self (unrealistic expectations)

### Behaviour & Usage
- **Tech Savvy:** High (uses Strava for parkrun, Spotify, productivity apps)
- **Swimming Frequency:** 2-3x per week
- **Session Length:** 1000-2000m per session
- **Data Source:** Garmin Swim 2 watch (uploads .FIT files)
- **Time of Day:** Early morning (6am) before work, or lunchtime

### What She'd Love in the App
- ✅ AI coaching insights that answer "am I doing alright?"
- ✅ Progress visualisation that proves improvement
- ✅ Personal records and badges (nostalgia from competitive days)
- ✅ Comparisons to "returning swimmers" not elite athletes
- ✅ Technique tips based on her data

### What Would Frustrate Her
- ❌ Too many technical metrics without context
- ❌ Feeling judged for "slow" times
- ❌ Complicated UI that takes ages to learn
- ❌ Features designed for competitive swimmers

### Key Quote
> "I used to swim a 1:05 100m at county level. Now I'm doing 2:30 and I don't know if that's pathetic or actually quite good for someone who hasn't trained in 15 years."

### Testing Checklist for Claire
- [ ] Does the UI explain what metrics mean?
- [ ] Are there age-appropriate benchmarks?
- [ ] Is the tone encouraging, not judgemental?
- [ ] Can she understand her progress in < 30 seconds?

## Persona 2: Data-Driven David
**The Analytical Optimizer**

### Demographics
- **Age:** 42
- **Location:** Manchester
- **Occupation:** Software Engineer
- **Swimming Background:** Started 2 years ago for parkrun cross-training, loves tracking everything

### Goals & Motivations
- Optimize training efficiency (limited time, maximum results)
- Find patterns (time of day, rest days, nutrition effects)
- Hit specific pace/distance targets
- Compare progress to benchmarks and himself over time

### Pain Points & Frustrations
- Current apps show data but don't give actionable insights
- Too many metrics, doesn't know which matter most
- Wants to ask "why was my pace slower this week?" and get answers
- Struggles to spot trends in noisy data
- No API access to his own data

### Behaviour & Usage
- **Tech Savvy:** Very High (early adopter, builds side projects, comfortable with APIs)
- **Swimming Frequency:** 3-4x per week
- **Session Length:** 2500-3500m per session
- **Data Source:** Apple Watch + manual CSV exports
- **Time of Day:** Varies (experiments with morning vs. evening)

### What He'd Love in the App
- ✅ Natural language querying (Ask AI feature is perfect)
- ✅ Detailed analytics and trend charts
- ✅ Pattern recognition and correlations
- ✅ Data export (CSV, JSON, API access)
- ✅ Custom metric calculations
- ✅ A/B testing different training approaches

### What Would Frustrate Him
- ❌ Can't customise dashboard
- ❌ Limited export options
- ❌ No API/integration capabilities
- ❌ Shallow analytics (just averages)
- ❌ Can't track external factors (sleep, nutrition)

### Key Quote
> "I want to know: do I swim faster on days when I sleep 8+ hours? After rest days? In the morning vs. evening? Show me the data, let me explore it."

### Testing Checklist for David
- [ ] Can he query complex questions?
- [ ] Are advanced features discoverable?
- [ ] Can he export his data easily?
- [ ] Does the app reveal patterns automatically?

## Persona 3: Milestone Maria
**The Goal-Oriented Beginner**

### Demographics
- **Age:** 28
- **Location:** Leeds
- **Occupation:** Nurse (night shifts, irregular schedule)
- **Swimming Background:** Never swam laps until 6 months ago, training for first Olympic triathlon

### Goals & Motivations
- Build endurance safely (prevent shoulder injury)
- Track progress toward 1500m continuous swim goal
- Celebrate small wins to stay motivated
- Learn proper technique from data insights (can't afford a coach)

### Pain Points & Frustrations
- Every swim feels hard, can't tell if she's improving
- Doesn't know if her SWOLF score is good or bad (what even is SWOLF?)
- Gets discouraged comparing to others at pool
- Needs encouragement and concrete milestones
- Often forgets to start watch before swimming

### Behaviour & Usage
- **Tech Savvy:** Medium (uses Instagram, basic fitness apps, not super technical)
- **Swimming Frequency:** 2x per week (wants to do 3x, struggles with consistency)
- **Session Length:** 800-1500m per session
- **Data Source:** Garmin watch (sometimes forgets to press start)
- **Time of Day:** Variable (after night shifts, weekends)

### What She'd Love in the App
- ✅ Achievement badges and celebrations for milestones
- ✅ Clear progress bars toward goals (50% to 1500m!)
- ✅ Encouraging AI coach tone (not technical)
- ✅ Visual improvement indicators (graph going up!)
- ✅ Educational content about swimming basics
- ✅ "You're doing great!" positive reinforcement

### What Would Frustrate Her
- ❌ Technical jargon without explanations
- ❌ Overwhelming charts and numbers
- ❌ Unclear what pace to target as a beginner
- ❌ Comparing herself to experienced swimmers
- ❌ Features that assume swimming knowledge

### Key Quote
> "I just want to know: am I getting better? Am I on track for my triathlon? And please tell me that 3:15/100m is alright for a beginner..."

### Testing Checklist for Maria
- [ ] Are metrics explained in simple terms?
- [ ] Does she feel celebrated for showing up?
- [ ] Can she find her "next milestone" easily?
- [ ] Is the tone encouraging, not overwhelming?

## Persona 4: Competitive Chris
**The Masters Swimmer**

### Demographics
- **Age:** 51
- **Location:** Edinburgh
- **Occupation:** Physiotherapist
- **Swimming Background:** Masters swimmer, competes in local galas 4-6x per year, very serious

### Goals & Motivations
- Shave seconds off 100m freestyle time
- Analyse lap-by-lap pacing strategy
- Track taper effectiveness before galas
- Monitor fatigue and recovery closely
- Compete successfully in age group (50-54)

### Pain Points & Frustrations
- Needs granular lap data, not just session averages
- Wants to compare sets (warm-up vs. main set vs. cooldown)
- Current tracking lacks race simulation features
- Needs structured workout logging (8x100 on 1:30, etc.)
- Missing periodisation planning tools

### Behaviour & Usage
- **Tech Savvy:** Medium-High (comfortable with tech, focused on swimming not gadgets)
- **Swimming Frequency:** 5-6x per week
- **Session Length:** 3000-5000m per session
- **Data Source:** Garmin watch with structured workouts pre-loaded
- **Time of Day:** Morning masters practice (5:30am), afternoon solo sessions

### What He'd Love in the App
- ✅ Lap-by-lap breakdown with split times
- ✅ Pacing strategy analysis (negative split detection)
- ✅ Workout builder with intervals
- ✅ Race/gala performance tracking
- ✅ Taper tracking and analysis
- ✅ Training zones by pace
- ✅ Age group comparison standards

### What Would Frustrate Him
- ❌ Can't log structured workouts (sets/intervals)
- ❌ No split time tracking (50m, 100m splits)
- ❌ Missing stroke rate data
- ❌ No gala performance features
- ❌ Simplified metrics that lack detail

### Key Quote
> "I need to know: was my pacing strategy effective? Did I start too fast? How does this compare to my age group at the last gala?"

### Testing Checklist for Chris
- [ ] Can he see lap-by-lap details?
- [ ] Can he log structured workouts?
- [ ] Are advanced metrics available?
- [ ] Does it support his competitive goals?

## Persona 5: Casual Casey
**The Wellness Swimmer**

### Demographics
- **Age:** 38
- **Location:** Brighton
- **Occupation:** Stay-at-home parent (3 kids)
- **Swimming Background:** Recreational only, swims for stress relief and gentle exercise

### Goals & Motivations
- Maintain consistent habit (proud of showing up)
- Feel accomplished without pressure or competition
- Track streaks and frequency more than speed
- Mental health benefit (meditation in motion)
- Simple interface that doesn't overwhelm

### Pain Points & Frustrations
- Most swim apps are too complicated/competitive
- Doesn't care about pace times, just wants recognition for swimming
- Feels judged by performance metrics
- Needs positive reinforcement, not optimization pressure
- Intimidated by athletic/competitive features

### Behaviour & Usage
- **Tech Savvy:** Low-Medium (prefers simple, beautiful apps like Headspace)
- **Swimming Frequency:** 1-2x per week (goal is consistency, not performance)
- **Session Length:** 500-1200m per session
- **Data Source:** Basic fitness tracker or manual entry
- **Time of Day:** Mid-morning (after kids at school) or evening

### What She'd Love in the App
- ✅ Streak tracking ("5 swims in a row!")
- ✅ Celebratory animations for showing up
- ✅ Non-judgemental, gentle tone
- ✅ Focus on consistency over performance
- ✅ Beautiful, calming design
- ✅ Option to hide pace/performance metrics
- ✅ Gratitude/reflection prompts

### What Would Frustrate Her
- ❌ Too much emphasis on speed/getting faster
- ❌ Intimidating graphs and charts
- ❌ Feeling pressure from metrics
- ❌ Competitive language ("beat your best!")
- ❌ Complex features she doesn't need

### Key Quote
> "I don't care if I'm fast or slow. I just want to celebrate the fact that I made it to the pool twice this week despite everything going on."

### Testing Checklist for Casey
- [ ] Can she use the app without feeling judged?
- [ ] Are performance metrics optional/hidden?
- [ ] Does it celebrate showing up, not just performance?
- [ ] Is the interface simple and calming?

## How to Use This Document

1. **Before building a feature:** Check which personas would care about it
2. **Whilst designing:** Review relevant persona pain points and goals
3. **After building:** Test the feature as each relevant persona
4. **When prioritising:** Count how many personas need the feature

**Pro Tip:** Print out persona cards and keep them at your desk. Refer to them constantly!
